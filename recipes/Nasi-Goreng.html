What is Nasi Goreng?

The literal translation of Nasi Goreng is “fried rice” in Indonesian and Malaysian – and that’s exactly what it is! It’s mainly rice with just a little bit of meat and just onion for the vegetables.

Ingredients

    Chicken

  • 1 tbsp oil
  • 5 oz / 150g chicken breast
  • 1 tbsp kecap manis

    • Curry

  • 1.5 tbsp oil
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 1 tsp red chilli
  • 1 onion
  • 3 cups cooked white rice
  • 2 tbsp kecap manis
  • 2 tsp shrimp paste

    • Garnishes

  • 4 eggs
  • 1 green onion
  • Tomatos and cucumbers
  • Fried shallots
  • Lime wedges

Instructions

  1. Heat oil in a large skillet or wok over high heat.
  2. Add chilli and garlic, stir for 10 seconds.
  3. Add onion, cook for 1 minute.
    4. the wetness has reduced and the spice paste darkens
  4. Add chicken, cook until it mostly turns white, then add 1 tbsp kecap manis and cook for a further 1 minute
    5. or until chicken is mostly cooked through and a bit caramelised.
  5. Add rice, 2 tbsp kecap manis and shrimp paste, if using. Cook, stirring constantly, for 2 minutes until sauce reduces down and rice grains start to caramelise (key for flavour!).
  6. Serve, garnished with garnishes of choice (green onions, red chilli, fried shallots).
